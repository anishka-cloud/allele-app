## CHANNEL STRATEGY RATIONALE

### Why TikTok First (Not Pinterest)

Pinterest is Nish's strongest channel (124K impressions), but for THIS product:

1. **Speed to revenue:** TikTok videos can go viral within hours. Pinterest pins take weeks to gain traction. For a launch, you need fast signal on what resonates.
2. **TikTok Shop integration:** Direct in-app purchase with basket tags. No redirect friction. Pinterest requires users to click out to allele.app, then click ShopMy, then buy.
3. **Content format match:** Color analysis is inherently visual and dramatic -- the "reveal" format is native to TikTok, not Pinterest.
4. **Testing ground:** 5 TikToks in Week 1 gives you data on which hooks work. That data informs which Pinterest pin angles to double down on.

### Why Pinterest Second (Not Ignored)

1. **Compounding traffic:** Every pin is a permanent quiz funnel entry point
2. **SEO leverage:** "True Autumn color palette" and "best makeup for warm skin" are high-volume, low-competition Pinterest search terms
3. **Existing audience:** 124K impressions on The Nish Edit means immediate distribution
4. **No content creation overhead:** Season palette pins can be generated from the season data already in the app (hex colors, best/avoid lists)

### Why Instagram is Cross-Post Only

Instagram Reels get 10-20% of TikTok's organic reach for new accounts. The only reason to post there is: (a) cross-posting is free, and (b) some quiz-takers will share their results on IG Stories, which is free viral distribution.
